An Image Encryption System by Cellular Automata with Memory

IEEE-Third International Conference on Availability, Reliability and Security

F. Maleki, A. Mohades, S. M. Hashemi, and M. E. Shiri

In this paper, we proposed a lossy image encryption system using cellular automata with memory. The proposed method takes advantage of steganography concepts to offer a secure image encryption system resistant to cryptanalysis attacks.

A CA Randomizers Based on parallel CAs with Balanced Rules

International Conference on Computational Science, ICCS 2010

F. Maleki, A. Bijari, A. Mohades, and M.E. Shiri

In this paper, we defined a class of cellular automata rules for building random number generators based on cellular automata. We constructed and evaluated a non-uniform CA randomizer using the proposed rules. We used a battery of statistical tests to assess the quality of generated random numbers produced by the CA randomizer. The experimental results suggested that the proposed rules lead to high-quality random numbers.

Rule discovery for pseudorandom number generator based on cellular automata

IEEE-International Symposium on Information Technology

F. Maleki, A. Bijari, A. Mohades, and M. E. Shiri

The behaviour of a cellular automaton is determined by its rules; therefore, finding appropriate rules for a specific application is essential. In this paper, we used a genetic algorithm for rule discovery in cellular automata (CA) pseudorandom number generators. In addition, we introduce and prove the convergence theorem in a particular type of cellular automata.

Evolutionary approach for developing fast and stable offline humanoid walk

3rd Joint Conference of AI & Robotics and 5th RoboCup Iran Open International Symposium (RIOS), 2013

H. Farazi, F. Ahmadinejad, F. Maleki, M. E. Shiri

In this paper, we used an evolutionary approach to model the human walk for Nao, a standard humanoid robot. The proposed method made use of inverse kinematics for trajectory generations and led us to a superior forward velocity comparing to the other well-stablished methods.
